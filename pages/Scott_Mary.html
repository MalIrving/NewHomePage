Mary Scott (1852-1937)

Biography

Born, Newbie Barns, Annan, Dumfriesshire, Scotland. Father, John Scott, mother, Mary Dalrymple.

Birth of son, Thomas, Newbie Barns, Annan, Dumfrieshire, Scotland.

abt. 1874

Birth of daughter, Johann Scott, Annan, Dumfrieshire, Scotland.

25th July 1877

Notes

No record of birth certificate, but definitely before 1855, probably 1852 or 3. Marriage certificate 25th June 1875 at Annan Parish Church, her age 22 years. Death certificate: Mary Johnston, widow of William, on 6th April 1937, at 6 Wellington St. Annan. (daughter's home) aged 84 years, of gangrene and senility.


She certainly had a most eventful life, and a very hard one. Despite her record, it appears there were just the two loves in her life. We found that the first illig. quite by accident, as we were assuming that all her children were Irving's. Making enquires at Dumfries Library about fatal drowning in the River Annan, a microfiche of the local paper report gave a surprise, but enormous help regarding dates. The second illig. turned up by the way of Mary's death cert. It having been registered by Johnann McBryde, illegitimate daughter, but we weren't able to cross check due to lack of time. However, as Thomas Irving married her a year or two later, she was likely another of his!


Before marriage she had been a domestic servant, and during her widowhood took washing in, and at the time of her second marriage was working in the grain mill. It seems that her life after Thomas's death, was spent in North St., Annan, No 21 was her address on re-marriage and No 26 the address of at least two of her children on their marriages. Tom and Freda remember No 26 with great affection when they went to see their Granny. (Tom says that she had a bottom lip that quivered and frightened him through). We went to see the house but it has been replaced by an ugly concrete block of pensioners flats. However, No 24 is still there: see Cereal House on map, and this was obviously the home of the Provost Mill owner: R. Robinson (there was a plaque of a shield with RR inscribed). The houses around must have been built for their employees, and are of a very high standard for those days, and as you can see from the photo of me looking frozen outside No 34 they are still very nice little homes. I wonder if Mary had permanent work at cereal House, whilst trying to raise her children, it would explain how she had the house for such a very long time. The outlook to the front is exactly the same as on the map, just an open field and then the river, so it is likely that her son Thomas had gone there for a bath rather than a bathe.


Now to her third illig. Williamina Blaycock Irving - does the William part have any significance? Also where did the supposedly adopted William come from - the family were told that he was the child of a neighbour or a cousin. We do know quite definitely that she married William Johnston. Marriage certificate: 22, Feb, 1895, at 11 Johnson St, Annan, with the rites of the Church of Scotland, William Johnston, lorryman, bachelor, aged 37 of 7 Queensbury St., Annan to Mary Irving, grain mill worker, widow, aged 40 years, 21, North St. Annan. (Well telling fibs about one's age to get a toy boy is nothing new!) We did not pursue the Johnston line, he must have died before 1920. Quite something to take on a middle aged widow and her 7 surviving children, this makes me think that the last two could be his, as well as their Christian names. Mary does not have much idea in naming her illig. I have never seen spellings like JOHNann or WILLIAMina before, that I feel she was pointing the way to their fathers.


She was very close to Johnann, (she was the only child to stay in Annan,) and spent her final senile years with her, in the boarding house in Wellington Street.


She was in her 70's when Tom remembers her, and he says that she was always immaculate in appearance and had a house that always gleamed with cleanliness.


Notes from Catherine Milburn
