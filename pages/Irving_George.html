Date Action
11/08/1914 Attested at Victoria Barracks, Sydney New South Wales.

13/01/1916 Awarded 3 days C.B. and fined 10/-.
03/04/1916 Transferred to Anzac Police Corps from 3rd Battalion. T.O.S. of the Anzac Provost Corps from 3rd Battalion.
12th May 1916 21 Officers and 589 other ranks, selected from all units of the A.I.F., were marched into barracks Abbassia. Thus began one month of intensive training in all aspects of military police duties and soldiering. Those men who failed to meet the rigid requirement of the new Corps, were marched out, and returned to their units. From inception it had been decided that only A class men would be accepted into the new Corps. Also to eliminate the perception amongst other units in the A.I.F., that men joining the new Corps were doing so to evade Active Service. Those men who had not seen service in the face of the enemy were transferred to the Desert Mounted Corps, on completion of their training. Commanded by, Major General H.G. Chauvel CB, CMG., they remained with the Desert Corps, until they had seen service in the face of the enemy, and were capable of taking their place with the ANZAC Provost Corps (APC). After the initial intake, all new recruits must have seen service in the face of the enemy. By 1917, the requirements for entry into the APC were the strictest of any unit in the A.I.F.

16/08/1916 Wounded in action, France.
"Day quiet and some rain fell. We were subjected to heavy retaliation by trench mortars and rifle grenades in reply to our rifle grenades and Stokes mortar. This lasted from 2200 till 2330 when fire ceased quite suddenly. Very slight damage done as most of the projectiles fell harmlessly into the railway cutting on our right. A new listening post was established in front of left company in an old crator. Work on improving trenches continued, Drainage, duck boards and rebuilding parapet chiefly. Casulties 1 other rank killed 9 O.R. wounded." 3rd Battalion Diary's.

Biography

1883/11/05 - Born, Newbie Barnes, Dunfrieshire, Scotland. Father Thomas Irving, mother Mary Scott.

1884/07/21 - Father, Thomas Irving dies from Rheumatic Fever, Black Hills, Annan, Dunfrieshire. George was just 8 months old.

1891/04/05 - Occupation Scholar. Residence, 25 North Street, Annan, Dunfrieshire, Scotland. (1891 Scotland Census.)

1895/02/22 - George's mother, Mary remarries, William Johnston.

1898/08/20 - Commenced apprenticeship with R. Robinson & Sons, Annan, as a Miller.

1901/03/31 - Occupation Miller (grain). Residence, 25 North Street, Annan, Dunfrieshire, Scotland. (1901 Scotand Census).

1904/02/07 - Commenced employement with Glasgow Tramways as a Conductor.

1904/06 - Promoted to motorman. (Driver).

1911/04/02 - Occupation Motorman Tramway. Residence, 45 McIntosh Street, Glasgow, Scotland.(1911 Scotland Census).

1911/08/11? - Memorandum
R. Robinson & Sons
Scotch Oatmeal Manufacturers
August 10th 11
To whom it may concern.
George Irving the bearer, was in our employment for six years. He served his apprenticeship as a miller here & also he had a large experience of farm work on the lands we possess.
During this time he gave every satisfaction in the mill & on the farm, he left nothing to be desired in the performance of his duty.
Being strictly teetotal and trustworthy we placed a good deal of faith in him & in the fewest possible words, he did not fail us.
He is of an obliging nature and anyone who engages him maybe sure of every satisfaction.
He left our employment to better his position & for no other reason.
We can, with confidence recommend him and whoever engages him may be assured of a faithful discharge of his duties.
R Robinson & Sons
P.S. The above George Irving, was in our employment from August 20th 1898 until May 1st 1904.
RR&Sons
At this time George Irving must have had plans to immigrate to New Zealand as above reference bears the "High Commissioner to New Zealand" stamp with date, 2nd August, 1911.

1912/07/04 - Testimonial
Corporation Tramways 46 Bath Street Glasgow
George Irving has been employed in the services of the Glasgow Corporation Tramways from 7th February, 1904, until this date. He began as a Conductor, but after training as a Motorman, transferred in June 1904 to the steady driving list, and has acted as a Motorman since that date, occasionally taking a Conductor’s duty.
I have great pleasure in certifying that Irving has given excellent service ti the department and has proved to be, in every respect, a trustworthy, competent and sober man. He resigns from the service entirely of his own accord.
Irving has qualified regularly during the last seven years for the bonus which is paid to Motormen for freedom from accident.
We do not, as a rule, give certification to servants leaving the Department, but an exception is made in Irving’s case as he is going to Australia.
James Dalrymple General Manager 4th July, 1912.

DEATHS.

IRVING, George (Scottie). - Ex - 3rd Battalion A. I. F., October 23, 1970, at Concord Repatriation Hospital, late of 113 Caledonian Street, Bexley, dearly loved husband of the late Jean Irving, loved father of Thomas and William, fond father-in-law of Olive and Ethel, dear grandfather of the children, aged 87 years. At rest.
