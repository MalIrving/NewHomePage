William Irving

Biography

Forman in the construction and maitenance of Flour Mills

7th October, 1981

Death from a brain tumor at the Sutherland District Hospital, Caringbah, New South Wales.

12th October, 1981

Cremated, Woronora Cemetery, Sutherland, New South Wales.

William was born on the 27th December, 1923, at the Royal Womens Hospital, Paddington, New South Wales.

He was the second son of George Birrell Irving and Jean Grey Reid of 113 Caledonian Street, Bexley, New South Wales.

Bill as he was called, was educated at the Hurstville Public School and Hurstville Technical High School.

After leaving school, he took up an apprenticeship as a Pattern Maker.

On his marriage in 1947, Bill and his young family lived at his parents, whilst clearing and beginning to build the family home on a bush block in Oyster Bay.

The residence was designed by his brother, Thomas, an architech working for the water board.

Bill worked weekends and once the framework was completed moved the family into a garage on the property to reduce travelling time.

It took three years living in the garage until the house was completed. There were other families in the area carrying out the construction of their homes and the men would combine their efforts to get the work done. This resulted in friendships lasting lifetimes.

Bill died 7th October, 1981 after a short illness from a brain tumor at the Sutherland Hospital, Caringbah, New South Wales.

He was cremated and his ashes were interred in the rose garden in the Woronora Cemetery, Sutherland, New South Wales.
