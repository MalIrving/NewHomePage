Mary Johnson (1799-1845)

Biography

11th June, 1821

Mary marries Marmaduke Leeson in All Saints Church, Brocklesby, Lincolnshire, England.

4th March, 1822

Baptism of daughter, Elizabeth, Brocklesby, Lincolnshire, England.

23rd January, 1824

Baptism of son, Robert, Brocklesby, Lincolnshire, England.

March, 1826

Baptism of son, William, Brocklesby, Lincolnshire, England.

30th December, 1827

Birth of son, Christopher, Brocklesby, Lincolnshire, England.

24th October, 1829

Birth of son, Henry, Brocklesby, Lincolnshire, England.

19th May, 1833

Birth of son, Thomas, Lincolnshire, England.

4th December, 1834

Birth of son, Septimus, Wrawby, Lincolnshire, England.

9th December, 1834

Baptism of son Septimus, St. Mary's Wrawby, Lincolnshire, England.

22nd August, 1836

Birth of son, George, Wrawby, Lincolnshire, England.

16th September, 1836

Baptism of son, George, Wrawby, Lincolnshire, England.

16th May 1844

With family as well as Elizabeth, Catherine, Frances and Anne Purcell under his protection, sail from Cork, Ireland aboard the sailing ship “Templar”, under the free immigration program to Australia. Under the immigration program, immigrants escourting young women under their protection where granted free passage.

18th July 1844

Daughter Elizabeth dies from Phthisis and is buried at sea.

27th August 1844

Family arrive Sydney, Australia.

31st August, 1844.

Sydney, Australia. Disposal List No. 35, 36, 132, 133, 134 & 135. Memorandum of Agreement made this day between William Bowman , Esquire of Richmond of the one part, and Marmaduke Leeson and sons free immigrants per ship Templar of the other part. The conditions are, that the said Marmaduke Leeson and sons engage to serve the said William Bowman as Farm Labourers or Shepards and otherwise make themselves generally useful, for the term of 12 months; and also to obey all his, or his overseers' or authorised agents' lawful and reasonable commands during that period; in consideration of which services the said William Bowman doth hereby agree to pay the said Marmaduke Leeson and Sons wages, at the rate of 50 pounds per annum, and to provide them with the following rations weekly: - It being specially and expressly understood that the said William Bowman shall have the services of the entire family of the said Marrnaduke Leeson and for which the above sum of 50 pounds is to be the remuneration in full the ..... to commence from the date of arrival at Richmond.
Beef or Mutton........ 50 pounds.
Flour....................... 60 pounds.
Sugar........................ 6 pounds.
Tea ........................... 1 pound.
In witness where they have mutually affixed their signatures to this document Witness..
William Bowman C. Morgan.
Marmaduke Leeson. We agree to subscribe to the above Robert Leeson. William Leeson. Christopher Leeson.

Marmaduke like so may other immigrants may have learnt to read and write on the long voyage out as he signed his name to the immigration papers but used his mark on his marriage certificate. His signiture on the immigration papers was M Duke Leeson, so he may have gone by the nickname Duke. This may have led to the confusion in many researchers that he was a Duke attached to the Irish brewery family.

3rd October, 1845

Died, Benevolent Asylum, Sydney, New South Wales, Australia.

October, 1845

Buried, Devonshire Street Cemetery, Sydney, New South Wales.

1901

Devonshire Street Cemetery closed and unclaimed bodies were reinterred in the Botany Cemetery, this was undertaken to construct Central Station. This is certainly the case as all of her children had moved to the Clarence River area 40 years before.
