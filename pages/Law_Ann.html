Ann Law (1834-1870)

Biography

27th June, 1834

Ann born Banff, Banffshire, Scotland.
Parents, John Law and Ann Wilson.

2nd June, 1841

The Voyage and Arrival in Australia.
On 2nd June, 1841, Ann and John Law, together with their three children, Ann, James, and Barbara, left Dundee in the barque, "Mathesis." for Sydney, Australia. Whilst crossing the Atlantic Ocean the ship's hold caught fire and the pumps had to be used continually until the ship reached Rio de Janeiro. As a result of the fire the family had to spend three months in Rio while the ship was repaired. Barbara, the youngest, had to be watched very carefully as she was a beautiful child and the Moors (from Nth. Africa) wanted her, and, because her paremts would not sell her they had to be careful that the Moors did not take her. The voyage from Rio resumed on 24th November, 1841. The family arrived in Sydney 30th January, 1842. From Sydney they continued their journey to the Wiliams River district where they settled to farm work near Dungog. In 1859 the family moved to the Clarence River and lived at Ulmarra for a few years before taking up land at Kings Creek, near Lawrence in 1862.
Compiled from the notes kept by Annie Georgina Law, The Law Family of the Nineteenth Century - Fay Manfield.

7th January, 1852

Marries Henry Leeson, Raymond Terrace, New South Wales.
Both Henry and Ann give their address as Nelsons's Plains, New South Wales in the marriage register.
They must have been living close to each other. They both signed the register with their mark 'x'.

26th September 1857

October 1857

Henry and Ann and their family move from the Hunter River to Ulmarra aboard the ship "Grafton". They are the first members of the family to do so. - We know that Ann's sister Barbara visited Ann and Henry soon after they went to live at Ulmarra, so, it is not difficult to imagine her returning home to her parents, John and Ann, with encouraging reports of the new, developing area, persuading the family to move their place of residence to the Clarence River. This move occurred on February, 1859 following severe flooding on the William's River area of the Hunter River valley.

1867

The family moves from Ulmarra to a new home called "Highlands" at Southgate. The move was undertaken with the hope of improving the Ann's health as she was suffering from T.B.

29th July, 1867

Baby Mary Ellen dies at Southgate just after the families move.

25th May, 1868

Birth of daughter, Kezia Emily, Southgate, New South Wales.

8th November, 1870

Death of wife Anne, at her residence, Southgate, New South Wales from consumption.

DEATH.
LEESON: - On the 8th of November, at her residence, Southgate, Clarence River, Ann, the beloved wife of Henry Leeson, aged thirty-six years, after a long and painful illness, leaving a husband and a large family to mourn their loss.
Extract from the "Daily Examiner, November 15th, 1870."

9th November, 1870

Ann buried in Church of England section, Grafton Cemetery, Grafton, New South Wales.
